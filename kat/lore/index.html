introduction

idk, i had doubts whether to add this, but i wanted to try out the ideas that aninternetuser0 suggested, and thats that. i know i yap about this quite a lot, but oh well. :D it is what it is.

anyhow, fake eurobeat [as it is called online for some reason] is actually disco polo, a polish disco genre, that originated in the 80s/90s with elements of western dance music elements [if i got it right].

usually, love and similar themes are the main ones in the genre. however, in poland, this genre is lowkey looked down upon, and is usually considered too simple or bland, which i personally understand. [but guess who likes vibey music...]

my experience, importance

personally, i always liked upbeat music that wasnt too harsh, but also not too soft, and i used to be a listener of phonk, another genre i really love, however, phonk didnt have any influence at the time of this website's creation, so i think eurobeat is more worthy of such a mention, anyhow. i first stumbled upon the 1st fake eurobeat mix during 2022 august, where i really started to like "mig ~ co ty mi dasz".

for a while after that, i forgot about eurobeat and the mix itself, i didn't like most songs on there, and just moved on. however, at some point around 2025 april, i decided to download the 1st mix again for no reason [check below for the links to the mixes, including the first one], i mean, there was a reason, i wanted to listen to car-related music, and guess what! the genre was not entirely directed at cars, but it fit the car theme pretty well, so i listened to it once or twice since then.

until.. in may of 2025, i decided to try to take a theory test for a car [license], and guess what, i actually passed after a whole day of listening to eurobeat and grinding the theory test questions lmao. and it was crazy, i passed first try [two times in a row, for a moped and now a car].

after that, eurobeat slowly grew to become my day-to-day music, as it was vibey, pretty uplifting and just energetic, which at the time, was the vibe! and well, with time, that genre grew to be more than just a genre. why?

its pretty simple, it was awesome and i loved it, thats about it lmao, but at the time, it lwokey felt like a lot of things went right, and then a few katrewkate beamng videos dropped, where most clips had eurobeat playing in the background, and i guess that added to the vibe and lowkey made me like the genre even more.

around that time, other events also worked out in my favor, and then i was pretty happy, and guess wat, happy music + happy me = awesomeness. or, for you, not really :/ but to me, yes!!! and i guess i associate eurobeat with "great times", because of the above mentioned events.

fate [pesronal]

um, sadly, or not, i think eurobeat [for me] will meet the same fate as all previous genres and songs have. they will remain in my playlist and be remembered for probably the rest of my life at this point, but the songs and mixes will most likely remain there, not being listened to and just kind of abandoned in a sense. dont get me wrong, i love the genre, and i do listen to it still, but "nothing truly lasts forever.", so... yeah:( however, i think i will truly have fond memories of the genre, as it truly helped me be me, and also enjoy life just a bit more.

as of the 5th day of october 2025, eurobeat is slowly becoming phased out from my daily playlists, and the era itself has mostly ended since oct 2nd. what happened on oct second you might ask. well, i passed my practical exam for well b kategory vehicles [cars]. which means, i am an officially licensed driver and can well drive a car. itss a bit sad to see this era end, since its been hella fun and im really happy to have experienced it, but yea. time to go on the roads!
